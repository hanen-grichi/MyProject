We deal in our researches with reconfigurable wireless sensor networks under energy constraints, we propose:

  • Methodology

    A new methodology of RWSN named RWiN-Methodology to analyze, construct, develop and verify easily and automatically an RWSN system in order to minimize the energy consumption by the network. The gain of the RWiNMethodology is the automatically generated steps from the initial specification to the target code.

    Article

  • Multi-agent architecture

    A new zone-based multi-agent architecture for RWSN where a communication protocol is well defined for useful distributed reconfigurations. This architecture conforms to the meta-model already developed. We declare a set of agents to handle this architecture. We define an agent as a software entity to make autonomously the required decisions. We propose a controller agent that handles the reconfiguration strategies of the whole network and assigns a zone agent for each zone to control the local reconfiguration scenarios.

    Article

  • A timed automata (TA)

    This model verify a set of constraints according to user requirements if we want to keep our network alive as much as possible. We verify functional and temporal constraints for all reconfiguration forms.

    Article

  • ROCL Language

    A new validation language in order to control the verification complexity of reconfigurable systems. We propose an extension of the object constraint language (OCL), named reconfigurable OCL, in order to optimize the specification and validation of constraints related to different executions of dynamic systems.

    Article

  • Conditional reconfiguration

    A new solution for dynamic resizing and mobility of an RWSN under energy constraints, in order to increase the network lifetime. After applying the functional reconfiguration, we apply a conditional one (change of the dimension of zones and the position of mobile nodes) according to the total charge of the network.

    Article
